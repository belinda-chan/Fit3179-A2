The World Happiness Report is a landmark survey that ranks the happiness levels of 156 countries based on how happy their citizens perceive themselves to be.

In the 2020 edition, the World Happiness Report looks at six key variables to evaluate the levels of happiness around the world, which are: GDP per capita, social support, healthy life expectancy, freedom to make life choices, generosity, and freedom from corruption (World Happiness Report).

This visualisation aims to explore these variables, some of them from different perspectives, to see how they compare for the happiest nations.

Happiness around the world

Some countries are happier than others

The Happiest Countries

The happiest countries all have one thing in common

The most happiest countries are all located in Europe, more specifically, the Scandinavian region.

Finland is ranked first place as the happiest nation in the world.

Other Scandinavian countries ranked highly as well, with Denmark in second place, Iceland and Norway in fourth and fifth place, and Sweden as the seventh most happiest country.

Freedom of Speech

Scandinavian countries have high freedom of speech

Scandinavian countries rank highly for freedom of speech.

The World Press Freedom Index ranks countries based on an assessment of press freedom records every year. It looks at the amount of freedom that each country has for journalists, news organisations, and netizens, in addition to the efforts of the country’s authorities to respect this freedom (Wikipedia).

From 2019 to 2020, both Norway and Finland mainatined their rankings of first place and second place, while Denmark moved up from fifth to third place. Sweden dropped one position from third to fourth place.

Freedom From Corruption

Most Scandinavians can have trust in their government

Respondents from 38 OECD countries were asked whether or not they have confidence in their government in a 2020 survey measuring current wellbeing (OECD).

While 23 of the countries surveyed had less than half have their population having trust in their government, this was not the case for Scandinavian countries.

In fact, almost 60% of people in Iceland trust their government, and just over 80% in Norway trust their government!

GDP Per Capita

Most Scandinavian nations had a GDP above world average in 2020

In the economic climate of 2020, the global GDP fell by 3.59% compared to the previous year's figure.

However, in a year where most countries and experienced a decrease in GDP growth, the macroeconomies of Norway, Denmark, Finland and Sweden still performed relatively well in comparison, where their decline in GDP growth was smaller than that of Europe and the global economy as a whole.

While Iceland is the only exception its Scandinavian counterparts, this result is unsurprising as pandemic affected one of the most important sectors to the country, which is their tourism industry (Nordicstatistics.org).

Healthy Life Expectancy

Scandinavians have a very healthy life expectancy

In 2020, the average global life expectancy at birth was 75.6 for females and 70.8 for males (Worldometers).

People from Scandinavian countries have a higher longevity than this world average: Iceland ranks in 10th place in the list of top 100 countries by life expectancy - women live to 84.9 years on average, while men live up to 82.2 years on average.

Sweden and Norway finish up in the top 20 of the list, at 13th and 17th place.

Social Support

Most Scandinavians know somebody that they can count on

The OECD measures social support using a survey asking the question: “If you were in trouble, do you have relatives or friends you can count on to help you whenever you need them, or not?”

Out of the 32 OCED countries, people from Iceland experience the lowest levels of social deprivation, in which only 1.67% of people are likely to not have people around them that they can count on socially.

Sweden has the highest percentage of people being socially deprived out of the Scandinavian countries, at 6.44%. However, this is still fairly good compared other countries in the sample - take Mexico or Greece for instance!
